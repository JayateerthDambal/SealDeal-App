User Guide & FAQ

Your step-by-step guide to leveraging the full power of SealDeal.ai for your investment analysis.

STEP 01

Create a New Deal

From the main dashboard, click the "New Deal" button. Give your deal a unique name to create a dedicated workspace. This is where all your documents and analysis for that specific investment will live.

STEP 02

Upload Your Documents

Inside your new deal workspace, you'll find the Document Manager. Drag and drop all relevant files—pitch decks (.pdf, .pptx), financials (.xlsx, .csv), and text documents—into the upload zone. You can upload multiple files at once.

STEP 03

Initiate the AI Analysis

Once your documents are uploaded, click the "Run Comprehensive Analysis" button. Our AI will begin processing all the information, cross-referencing data, and preparing your detailed report. The deal status will update to "Processing".

STEP 04

Share & Export Insights

When the analysis is complete, share your findings with stakeholders. You can export a beautifully formatted, multi-page PDF report or send a custom report with only the sections you select directly via email.

STEP 05

Query Your Data Instantly

Need a specific data point? Use the floating Agent Seal widget. Ask natural language questions like "What is the LTV/CAC ratio for Test 8?" and get immediate answers, complete with the underlying SQL query for full transparency.

Frequently Asked Questions

SealDeal.ai currently supports PDF (.pdf), PowerPoint (.pptx), Excel (.xlsx), CSV (.csv), and plain text (.txt) files. You can upload multiple files of different types for a single analysis.

We take data security seriously. Your files are uploaded to a private, secure Google Cloud Storage bucket associated only with your account. All analysis is performed within a secure cloud environment, and we leverage Firebase's robust security rules to ensure only you can access your data.

Our AI is specifically prompted to cross-reference information across all provided documents. If it finds inconsistencies (e.g., a different LTV in the pitch deck vs. the financial model), it will flag this as a "Key Risk Area" in the final analysis report for your review.
